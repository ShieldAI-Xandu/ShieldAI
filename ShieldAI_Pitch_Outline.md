# ShieldAI — Investor Pitch Deck Outline
### ~12 slides · pairs with the 10–15 min demo · mixed audience

> **How to use this:** This is the slide-by-slide skeleton. Each slide lists the
> headline, what goes on it, and the one thing it must make the investor feel.
> Keep slides sparse — a few words and one visual each. You talk; the slide supports.
> The demo slots in at Slide 6. Brackets `[ ]` mark numbers only you can fill in.

---

## Slide 1 — Title / Hook
**On the slide:** ShieldAI logo · one-line tagline · your name + contact
**Tagline option:** *"The virtual CISO every small business can finally afford."*
**Make them feel:** This is serious and clear from second one.
**Say:** Your name, what ShieldAI is in one sentence. Don't explain yet — just frame.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem
**Headline:** *Every business is a target. Almost none can afford protection.*
**On the slide (3 bullets max):**
- A full-time CISO costs **$200K–$400K/year**
- 99% of US businesses are small/mid-sized — they can't afford that
- Yet insurers, customers, and regulators now *require* a security program
**Make them feel:** There's a painful, widespread gap — and pressure forcing action.
**Visual:** A simple "too big to ignore / too small to afford" gap graphic.

---

## Slide 3 — Why Now
**Headline:** *The pressure just became unavoidable.*
**On the slide:**
- Cyber-insurance now demands proof of controls to issue/renew policies
- Big customers push security questionnaires down to small vendors
- Regulation expanding: HIPAA, CMMC, state privacy laws
- AI finally makes expert-level guidance deliverable at low cost
**Make them feel:** The market is being *created right now* — timing is the opportunity.
**Visual:** Rising-pressure timeline or four converging arrows.

---

## Slide 4 — The Solution
**Headline:** *ShieldAI — a virtual CISO, powered by AI, overseen by engineers.*
**On the slide:**
- Continuous security program, not a one-time PDF
- Real, defensible security score (NIST-based)
- Ready-to-use policies, roadmap, training, compliance tracking
- Priced as a subscription, not a consulting engagement
**Make them feel:** This is the obvious answer to the problem on Slide 2.
**Visual:** Simple before/after — "drawer PDF" vs "living program."

---

## Slide 5 — How It Works
**Headline:** *Assessment → Real Score → Full Program → Ongoing Management*
**On the slide:** A clean 4-step flow.
- **Assess:** conversational intake + structured NIST checklist
- **Score:** deterministic NIST CSF engine (not AI guesswork)
- **Generate:** policies, roadmap, training, exec report
- **Manage:** continuous monitoring + expert review
**Make them feel:** There's real engineering here, and a logical pipeline.
**Visual:** The 4-step arrow flow. This slide tees up the demo.

## Appendix slides (don't present; have ready for Q&A)
- **Team** — your background + why you're the one to build this (have this even if it's just you)
- **Competition** — honest landscape: incumbents (expensive consultants), DIY tools, other AI entrants; where you sit
- **Financial projections** — 3-year, even if rough
- **Liability & compliance** — your human-review model, terms, scope
- **Detailed pricing / tiers**
- **The scoring methodology** — one slide on the NIST engine for technical investors

---

## Design notes
- **One idea per slide.** If you're reading paragraphs off a slide, cut it.
- **Consistent dark/professional theme** — matches the product's look (ShieldAI blues).
- **Numbers are sparse but real.** Empty `[ ]` brackets = homework before pitch day. Don't guess market figures live; investors verify.
- **The deck supports you; it isn't the pitch.** You + the demo are the pitch.
- **Slide count:** 12 core is right for 10–15 min with a demo. Fewer is fine; more is not.
